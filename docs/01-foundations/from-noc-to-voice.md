# From NOC to Voice Specialist

> **Status:** stub. To be written from personal experience, with
> generalizable lessons for engineers on a similar path.

This document will cover:

- The NOC as a school: what you learn there that compounds for the
  rest of your career, and what you should leave behind quickly.
- The transition from passive monitoring to active engineering: the
  habits, the resistance, the projects that mark the shift.
- Voice as a specialization within networking: why it pays well, why
  it has fewer practitioners than it should, and what makes it
  different from data networking.
- Skills that compound: protocol-level thinking, packet capture
  fluency, scripting, the discipline of reproducing problems.
- Skills that don't: vendor-specific GUIs, certifications without
  hands-on practice, "knowing the buttons".
- Inflection points: the project, the outage, the conversation that
  marked each step in the path.
- What I would tell my 25-year-old self.

If you have walked a similar path and want to contribute your own
version, see [CONTRIBUTING.md](../../CONTRIBUTING.md). Multiple voices
on this page would make it more useful, not less.
